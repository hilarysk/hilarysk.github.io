Learning

If someone would pay me to take classes, I'd stay in school forever.

For example, I built this site with parallax just so I could learn it. I jump at the chance to attend conferences, like Nebraska.Code(), pictured above. Public lectures are my jam.

At times, it's daunting to realize how much there is to yet to learn - in particular, about programming - but it's also thrilling.

Making connections

When I was a journalist, connections were invaluable: radio reporters on Facebook who gave equipment recommendations; data journalists on Twitter who helped troubleshoot API calls; that one guy from my college French class who put me in touch with the perfect source for a story.

In tech, I've started building some of those integral connections by attending local meetups like Omaha Coding Women.

Teaching

As the daughter and stepdaughter of teachers, I grew up with a love of not only gaining knowledge, but also sharing it.

Part of my job at Nebraska NPR was teaching other reporters about digital reporting tools; recently, I volunteered as a mentor at an Omaha CoderDojo and helped adolescent girls learn to code (including one named - not joking - "Ruby"). Even if you're new to a subject or process, the best way to learn is to teach.
